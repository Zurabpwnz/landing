Why there is no need to be afraid of unlimited TravelToken emission and what is the difference between TravelChain and Ethereum

TravelChain will be used by EVERY traveller (1.2 billion people in 2016)

  • - Token emission is unlimited
  • - New tokens will be spent on the popularization and filling of the platform. Every new token is backed by new data
  • - It is possible to alter the amount of “emission”. This action is performed by voting of every token holder
  • - Emission of new tokens is enough to motivate users to develop the platform, but at the same time it saves the deflationary model (amount of tokens per user is decreasing with time)
  • - Built-in algorithms for the motivation to hold the tokens:
    • a) TT Holder Rating (Travel Power) depends on the amount and time of tokens stored in the wallet. The more this index is the bigger reward user gets for the development of the platform
    • b) access to registry for the data analysis is limited by the amount of tokens in the wallet. Tokens are not being spent. Amount of queries is limited.
  • - up to 100 000 transactions per second
  • - transactions in the network are fee-free
  • - Growth of the token price starts when the amount of supply exceeds the amount of demand with the release of the first app in Q1 of 2018

Ethereum is used by the most of the cryproinvestors and ICO founders.

  • - Token emission is unlimited
  • - New tokens are spent on the maintenance of the network (mining)
  • - In 2017 with the average price of $300 for ETH, 3 billion dollars were spent on mining
  • - Impossible to change the amount of emission (only hard-fork)
  • - Price growth has started thanks to popularization of ICOs
  • - 30 transactions per second
  • - fee for transactions (gas)
  • - ETH has grown 2800 times since July 2015
  • -BTC has grown 1000 times since July 2015

Ethereum Classic is used by some cryptoinvestors and ICO founders

  • - ETC grew 150 times since July 2015

Let’s sum it up:

Today’s cryptocurrencies are the new generation of money

1st generation - centralized fiat money which is printed to one hands

2nd generation - cryptocurrency. Pseudo-decentralized. Printed to the hands of every miner. Miner’s mostly don’t care what coin do they mine.

3rd generation - cryptocurrency which will be printed to the hands of all people across the planet which have a smartphone. Tokens are issued for the actions performed for the benefit of the system, not for the burning of electricity supporting the code. First step in this direction was made by Steemit and Golos, but their audience is pretty narrow - bloggers. TravelChain aims for the travellers - 1.2 billion people in 2016 and this quantity grows year by year. What’s more, travellers are going to spread this info globally and TravelToken will be “mined” and used not only by the travellers. As we can see with the current price of the crypto-forks like ZCash and ZCash Classic, Ethereum and Ethereum classic, the more people use the coin, the higher is price. Travellers community is much bigger than community of investors and ICO founders using Ethereum. Thereby, TravelChain team is going to make a solid contribution to the global decentralization.
